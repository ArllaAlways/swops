Pricing Register Interest

Swops was created by a Melbourne renter for renters. We rent a beautiful family home in Preston and we pay a lot for the privilege. At Christmas 2025 we were going to Sydney for a holiday. We knew for certain that someone with a nice house up there would love to come to Melbourne for Christmas and stay at ours, so we asked friends, friends-of-friends and looked around different house-swapping sites.

But because we rent, house swapping wasn’t really available to us without creating a public profile on a cheesy site built for homeowners. Creating a profile with pics of our house meant potentially having to get into the weeds with our agent and landlord for what felt like something that we shouldn’t need to ask permission for. We’ve had friends sit our house and we’re quite capable of making that judgement call for ourselves.

So with no other option, we left our expensive home sitting empty for 12 days while we paid rent for it and also paid through the nose for a holiday rental in Dee Why. It seemed real dumb. So here we are, talking to you about making holidays not so dumb for renters.

Let’s get together and give each other a break.

SWOPS privately matches renters with renters for short stays in each other’s homes.

Register interest for a swop window > Get matched with someone in your city > Meet on video call, see the place > Swop… or don’t. Up to you.

We manually review every registration and match you based on your suburb, home type and what you’re after. If there’s no good match right now, we’ll let you know.

No public listings. No public profiles. Between renters, for renters.

A swop isn’t a luxury holiday. It’s waking up in St Kilda instead of Brunswick for a weekend and seeing what happens. It’s going to bed in Katoomba when your bedroom is in Bronte.

Because why not? You already paid for accom.

You’ve got enough lords in your life, so we’re staying right out of it.

The spirit of Swops is peer-to-peer reciprocity. We believe that mutual hospitality where both parties have skin in the game is a much stronger behavioural moderator than reviews.

Or in the words of Jay Z: “Treat people how you wanna be treated, that’s the code.”

But don’t take our word for it.

You should meet your match (on video call). Look them in the eye. Talk. Ask questions. Make your own judgement about whether it feels right to you.

That’s how getting a friend-of-a-friend to house-sit works. Sort of. Swops is the same thing. But better. It’s fast-friends turning the dead money of rent into free accommodation in a new scene.

If that sounds good to you then register your interest (no obligation) and get ready to get fully lost at sunset in someone else’s neighbourhood.
