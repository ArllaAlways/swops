This King’s Birthday, Melbourne renters are doing a Euro Slummer.

Australian home swapping platform for renters, Swops, opens registrations for “Euro Slummer” this King’s Birthday long weekend, 6–8 June 2026.

Euro Slummer is the tongue-in-cheek alternative for Melbourne renters who can’t get to Europe this Northern summer. Which, in 2026, is most of them.

Record rents and living expenses have eaten the holiday fund and a return ticket to Rome that cost $1,500 last year is now closer to $2,700.

So this King’s Birthday, instead of leaving their Brunswick home empty and paying through the nose for somewhere to stay, Melbourne renters can swop homes with each other for the long weekend and pretend. The first swop pairs Melbourne’s Inner North suburbs with those on the Mornington Peninsula.

The ’ninch is not the Amalfi coast, but The Pillars at Mt Martha look uncannily like it. In June you experience them in a puffer jacket and a howling Southerly. A home-delivered lasagne in a Brunswick terrace is not Rome. But if you close your eyes and chew, it could be. And at $0 for accommodation it’s also significantly cheaper and you don’t have a 22-hour flight home on Sunday night.

A third of Australians rent. Most of them spend a third of their income doing it. Every time they go away, their home sits empty and they pay again to rent somewhere else. Swops exists to interrupt that double spend, starting with cross-town Melbourne swops, with Sydney and the world next.

Swops was founded by John Pace, who runs the production company, Hooves, representing top directors from Australia and around the world. “I work with young creative people every day and I see how being smart, talented and always busting your gut doesn’t readily translate to financial and living security. But I also see that they’re hilarious, ironic, generous, resourceful and resilient, so I wanted to build something adventurous that somehow unlocks a positive from paying so much rent. Something fun and just for them.”

“But I should be clear,” adds Pace. “Swops isn’t some kind of class war sob story. It’s just a very simple idea sitting right under our noses that unlocks value from rent without depriving landlords of anything. It allows the rent that people are paying to fund a mini break. A fun change of scene. A mental deep breath. Who wouldn’t want that for hardworking young Australians?”

Swops operates as a matching service, pairing renters with renters for short reciprocal breaks in each other’s homes – across town and eventually across states. No money changes hands and privacy is core. There are no public photos or public profiles of homes, only text descriptions and a video call to meet, see the place and arrange the swop. It’s closer to private online dating than yet another short stay listing site.

“I’m very excited to see what renters do with Swops,” said Pace. “They deserve a break.”

Registration for Euro Slummer is open now. Places are limited and registration closes May 9. Swops is live at swops.it.
