This King’s Birthday, Melbourne renters are doing a Euro Slummer.

Australian home swapping platform for renters, Swops, opens registrations for its first swop - “Euro Slummer” - this King’s Birthday long weekend, 6–8 June 2026.

Euro Slummer is the tongue-in-cheek alternative for Melbourne renters who can’t get to Europe this Northern summer. Which, in 2026, is most of them.

Record rents and living expenses have eaten the holiday fund and a return ticket to Rome that cost $1,500 last year is now closer to $2,700.

So this King’s Birthday, instead of leaving their Brunswick terrace empty and paying through the nose for somewhere to stay, Melbourne renters can swop homes with each other for the long weekend. Inner North for Mornington Peninsula. Carlton for Brighton. Fitzroy for Mt Martha.

The Mornington Peninsula is not the Amalfi coast, but The Pillars at Mt Martha look uncannily like it. In June you experience them in a puffer jacket and a howling Southerly. A home-delivered lasagne in a Brunswick terrace is not Rome. But if you close your eyes and chew, it could be. And at $0 for accommodation it’s also significantly cheaper and you don’t have a 22-hour flight home on Sunday night.

These cross-town rental home swaps throughout Melbourne (and soon Sydney) are where Swops is beginning its mission to unlock the holiday value sitting dormant in the rent that a third of Australians spend a third of their income on.

Swops operates as a matching service, pairing renters with renters for short reciprocal breaks in each other’s homes. No money changes hands. There are no public photos or public profiles of homes, only text descriptions and a video call to meet, see the place and arrange the swop. It’s closer to private online dating than yet another short stay listing site.

Registration for Euro Slummer is open now. Swops is live at swops.it.
